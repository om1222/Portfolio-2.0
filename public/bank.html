The Problem

$435M portfolio — no reliable view

⚠️
Data Quality Issues
5 critical problems in raw loan records
I
Inconsistent loan status labels — "Current", "current", "CURRENT" treated as 3 separate categories, distorting every risk metric
II
NULL repayment dates — missing last_pymnt_d timestamps broke all MoM and MTD calculations
III
Mismatched funded vs received amounts — no reconciliation between disbursed and collected figures
IV
No bad loan classification — Charged Off and Late loans mixed with healthy ones, making $65.5M exposure invisible
🏛️
Strategic Blind Spots
What leadership couldn't see
I
No MoM loan growth view — monthly funding trend required manual extraction, consuming 15+ hours every month
II
No real-time bad loan alert — $65.5M in risk exposure (13.8% bad ratio) was invisible without a consolidated risk dashboard
III
No grade-level risk profiling — Grade D–G loans carrying 18–40% default rates not identified for pricing adjustments
IV
No MTD performance tracking — intra-month funding vs repayment status unknown until month close

Risk Insight
13.8% bad loan ratio = $65.5M exposure, flagged early. Requires stricter underwriting for Grade D+ borrowers and revised credit scoring thresholds to prevent further accumulation.
Portfolio Health
$473.9M repaid > $435.8M funded — $38.1M net yield (8.7%). Repayment growth outpacing loan growth signals improving borrower quality and portfolio momentum heading into next fiscal year.
Growth Trend
13.3% MoM loan growth and 15.8% MoM repayment growth both automated via LAG() window function — no manual calculation needed. Leadership receives this KPI daily without analyst intervention.
Strategic Lever
Debt consolidation = 45% of all loans and carries lower default rates than other purposes. Targeting this segment with competitive rates can expand the high-quality loan book while reducing overall portfolio risk.

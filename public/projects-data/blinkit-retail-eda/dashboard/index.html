01 / KEY INSIGHT
Low Fat = 65% of total sales ($780K). Inventory teams should maintain 2× stocking levels for Low Fat SKUs to avoid stockouts in this dominant category, directly shaping procurement and marketing strategy.
02 / CATEGORY
Fruits & Vegetables leads all categories, followed by Snack Foods and Household. These 3 together contribute ~37% of total sales — core focus for supply chain optimization and shelf placement.
03 / LOCATION
Tier 3 outlets generate the highest revenue share at 38.3% ($459K) — counter-intuitive to urban-first assumptions. Tier 3 expansion and marketing investment have a clear positive ROI signal.
04 / RATING
4.0/5 average rating is consistent across all outlet types. Rating 4 is the most common (50% of records), with a small tail at 1–2 stars. Stable customer satisfaction indicates strong brand trust.
05 / OUTLET
Supermarket Type 1 dominates revenue — benefiting from wider product range and larger footfall. Grocery Stores average lower per-outlet revenue but serve dense last-mile markets effectively.
06 / TREND
Outlets established in 1985 and 1999 show peak sales — mature, high-trust locations. Newer outlets (2004–2009) are on a growth curve, suggesting Blinkit's expansion strategy is gaining traction.

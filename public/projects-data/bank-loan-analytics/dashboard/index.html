01 — Bad Loans
13.8% bad loan ratio = $65.5M exposure, flagged early for risk mitigation. Requires immediate restructuring: stricter underwriting, revised borrower screening, and risk-adjusted pricing across Grade D–G.
02 — Repayments
$473.9M repaid > $435.8M funded — repayments exceed funding by $38.1M. This indicates healthy portfolio discipline and strong borrower repayment behavior, an important signal for portfolio expansion.
03 — MoM Growth
13.3% MoM loan growth and 15.8% MoM repayment growth confirm accelerating momentum. Repayment growth outpacing loan growth is a leading indicator of improving portfolio quality and borrower reliability.
04 — Automation
3 dashboards across Power BI, Tableau, and Excel with 15+ automated KPIs eliminated manual Excel reporting — saving ~15 hours/month for finance teams and enabling real-time decision-making for executives.
05 — Grade Risk
Grade A–B loans have <5% bad loan rates. Grade E–G loans carry 25–40% bad rates but only represent 14% of portfolio. Risk-adjusted pricing for lower grades can improve net yield without increasing exposure.
06 — Strategy
Debt consolidation (45% of loans) is the dominant purpose — these borrowers tend to have lower default rates. Targeted campaigns in this segment combined with credit score thresholds can optimize the portfolio mix.
